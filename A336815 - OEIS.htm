Hugo Angulo: In a newton's cradle algorithm, starting from a swing from the right side of the table, the neighboring element(ball) which is "striked" gets allotted a value. The transfer of value throughout the cradle is based on the value of each "ball" and direction of associativity. This addition of values that the "ball" adds on to the neighboring "ball" and the "ball" on the opposite side generates the fibonacci sequence, and while doing so, generates the provided sequence which is based on the number of "strikes" and the sum assigned to each column (ball). This sequence is adjacent to the fibonacci sequence in the way it is generated, but the sequence presented is the sum of associative transitivity within the guidelines of a newtons cradles neighboring "ball" and the values held in each column (ball). 

So its a newtons cradle that counts strikes and swings, adds a value to the neighbor, and a value to the opposite end. When the opposite end comes back to strike the neighboring ball, it adds the values together, and sends the opposite side into swing. Once swung back the value of the swung ball gets updated, alongside with its neighbor,  and follows through to swing the opposite side, and this continues. The surprising thing is the emergence of fibonacci using this method, and i believe the sum of transference is an important property as well

In this one-ball swing - five ball Newton's cradle, the swinging ball adds its own value to the hit ball and the ball at the opposite end on outward swing. Sum calculated on stationary balls, between double parenthesis in example.

EXAMPLE

For n = 1 the a(1, First) = 0 since the sum of position, ball[1-4] is 0. Ball[5] is lifted and beforenot releasestationary and therefore excluded from summands, ball[5] is given the ainitial value of 1.

For n = 2 the a(2) = 2, swinging since the sum of ball[1-5] is 2. Ball[5] hitsstrikes ball[4] and adds its 1value to ball[4].]. All five balls are considered stationary while the momentum and energy travels through the balls to the opposite side of the cradle and all balls are therefore included in summands.

For n = 3 the a(3, ) = 2 since the sum of ball[4-5] addsis 2. Ball[1] swings out and is added the value of toball[5], since ball[1] onis swinging, it is not stationary and not included in outwardthe swingsummands.

For n = 4 the a(4) = 4, since the sum of ball[1-5] is 4. Ball[1] hitsstrikes ball[2] and add its value of 1 to ball[2].]. All balls stationary.

For n = 5 the a(5) = 3 since, ball[1] adds 1 to ball[5] on outward swing and thus excluded from summands.

If you are not interested in mathematics then you are wasting your time in this community, because you have no feeling for what we would find interesting (not everyone finds every sequence interesting - just as not everyone has the same taste in music or art). I can only suggest you run searches in oeis to find things you yourself are interested in (things like 'keyword:new' and 'keyword:new keyword:more' are good ones), and when you find something interesting then that might give you something to build on. NAME

a(n) is the sum of the stationary balls' value in an algorithm, inspired by a one-ball swing, five-ball Newton's cradle.

FORMULA

a(n) for odd n = A001610 (conjectured);

a(n) for even n = A000071 (conjectured).

EXAMPLE

For n = 1 the a(1) = 0 since the sum of ball[1-4] is 0. Ball[5] is lifted and not stationary and therefore excluded from summands, ball[5] is given the initial value 1.

For n = 2 the a(2) = 2 since the sum of ball[1-5] is 2. Ball[5] strikes ball[4] and adds its value to ball[4]. All five balls are considered stationary while the momentum and energy travels through the balls to the opposite side of the cradle and all balls are therefore included in summands.

For n = 3 the a(3) = 2 since the sum of ball[4-5] is 2. Ball[1] swings out and is added the value of ball[5], since ball[1] is swinging, it is not stationary and not included in the summands.

For n = 4 the a(4) = 4 since the sum of ball[1-5] is 4. Ball[1] strikes ball[2] and add its value of 1 to ball[2]. All balls stationary.

For n = 5 the a(5) = 3 since the sum of ball[4-5] is 3, ball[1] adds its value of 1 to ball[5] on outward swing and ball[5] is thus excluded from summands.

For n = 6, cycle repeats on n = 2.

Hugo Angulo: In a newton's cradle algorithm, starting from a swing from the right side of the table, the neighboring element(ball) which is "striked" gets allotted a value. The transfer of value throughout the cradle is based on the value of each "ball" and direction of associativity. This addition of values that the "ball" adds on to the neighboring "ball" and the "ball" on the opposite side generates the fibonacci sequence, and while doing so, generates the provided sequence which is based on the number of "strikes" and the sum assigned to each column (ball). This sequence is adjacent to the fibonacci sequence in the way it is generated, but the sequence presented is the sum of associative transitivity within the guidelines of a newtons cradles neighboring "ball" and the values held in each column (ball). 

So its a newtons cradle that counts strikes and swings, adds a value to the neighbor, and a value to the opposite end. When the opposite end comes back to strike the neighboring ball, it adds the values together, and sends the opposite side into swing. Once swung back the value of the swung ball gets updated, alongside with its neighbor,  and follows through to swing the opposite side, and this continues. The surprising thing is the emergence of fibonacci using this method, and i believe the sum of transference is an important property as well
